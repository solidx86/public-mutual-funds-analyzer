# Suitability Assessment (SA) Guide

## Regulatory Basis
Under Securities Commission Malaysia (SC) guidelines and FIMM regulations, a Suitability Assessment
is **mandatory** before recommending any unit trust fund. This is not optional — UTCs who recommend
unsuitable funds face disciplinary action.

---

## SA Questionnaire

Present all 8 questions at once. Score each answer, then sum for risk profile.

### Questions & Scoring

**Q1. What is your primary investment objective?**
| Answer | Score | Maps To |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Capital preservation | 1 | Bond/Money Market focus |
| Regular income | 2 | Income equity + Bond |
| Balanced growth & income | 3 | Mixed Asset + Equity |
| Maximum capital growth | 4 | Equity focus |

**Q2. What is your investment time horizon?**
| Answer | Score | Maps To |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Less than 3 years | 1 | Max 30% equity |
| 3–5 years | 2 | Max 50% equity |
| 5–10 years | 3 | Max 75% equity |
| More than 10 years | 4 | Up to 90% equity |

**Q3. If your portfolio dropped 20% in value, what would you do?**
| Answer | Score | Maps To |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Sell everything immediately | 1 | Very low risk tolerance |
| Sell some to reduce exposure | 2 | Low risk tolerance |
| Hold and wait for recovery | 3 | Moderate risk tolerance |
| Buy more at the lower price | 4 | High risk tolerance |

**Q4. What is your approximate monthly income range?**
| Answer | Score | Maps To |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Below RM 3,000 | 1 | Conservative sizing |
| RM 3,000 – RM 7,000 | 2 | Moderate sizing |
| RM 7,000 – RM 15,000 | 3 | Growth sizing |
| Above RM 15,000 | 4 | Aggressive sizing |

**Q5. Do you have an emergency fund (3–6 months of expenses)?**
| Answer | Score | Maps To |
|--------|-------|---------|
| No emergency fund | 1 | Must allocate to money market first |
| Less than 3 months | 2 | Include money market component |
| 3–6 months | 3 | Standard allocation |
| More than 6 months | 4 | Full flexibility |

**Q6. What is your investment experience?**
| Answer | Score | Maps To |
|--------|-------|---------|
| None — first time investing | 1 | Detailed explanations needed |
| Some — have invested in fixed deposits or savings | 2 | Basic fund knowledge |
| Experienced — have invested in unit trusts or stocks | 3 | Concise data-driven |
| Very experienced — active portfolio management | 4 | Expert-level discussion |

**Q7. What is your age range?**
| Answer | Score | Maps To |
|--------|-------|---------|
| 55 and above | 1 | Shorter effective horizon |
| 45–54 | 2 | Medium horizon |
| 35–44 | 3 | Longer horizon |
| 20–34 | 4 | Longest horizon |

**Q8. Do you have a Shariah compliance preference?**
| Answer | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Yes — Shariah only | Filter to Shariah-compliant funds only |
| No preference | Include all funds |
| Conventional only | Exclude Shariah-only series |

> Q8 is a filter, not scored. It restricts the fund universe.

## Score Calculation

**Total score = Q1 + Q2 + Q3 + Q4 + Q5 + Q6 + Q7** (range: 7–28)

| Score Range | Risk Profile | Description |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| 7–11 | **Conservative** | Capital preservation priority. Minimal tolerance for loss. Prefers stability over growth. |
| 12–17 | **Moderate** | Balanced approach. Accepts some fluctuation for moderate growth. Wants a mix of safety and returns. |
| 18–23 | **Growth** | Growth-oriented. Comfortable with significant volatility. Focuses on medium-to-long-term capital appreciation. |
| 24–28 | **Aggressive** | Maximum growth. High tolerance for drawdowns. Long time horizon. Willing to concentrate in high-alpha equity. |

## Important SA Principles

1. **No recommendation before SA completion** — this is non-negotiable
2. **Client override** — if a client insists on funds outside their profile, document this explicitly:
   "Client has been advised that [Fund X] exceeds their risk profile. Client acknowledges and proceeds."
3. **Review periodically** — SA should be refreshed annually or when life circumstances change
   (marriage, children, job change, retirement, inheritance)
4. **Emergency fund first** — if Q5 = "No emergency fund", always recommend allocating to a
   money market fund (e.g., PMMF, PeMMF) first before any other investment
5. **Cooling-off** — remind first-time investors of the 6 business day cooling-off right
