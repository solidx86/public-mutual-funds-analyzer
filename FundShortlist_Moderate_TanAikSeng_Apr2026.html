New Investor Foundation
Plain-English concepts before we look at the funds

What is a Unit Trust?

Think of it like a shared grocery run. Hundreds of investors pool their money together. A professional fund manager uses that pool to buy a diversified basket of bonds, stocks, or other assets — assets that most individual investors couldn't afford or manage alone. You own a proportional share of the basket.

What is NAV?

NAV stands for Net Asset Value — the price per unit of the fund, recalculated every business day. If the investments inside the fund go up in value, the NAV rises. If they fall, it dips. Your investment is worth: units you hold × current NAV. Unlike stocks, NAV is not traded live — it is set once daily after market close.

What does "Beating the Benchmark" mean?

Every fund has a benchmark — the standard it is measured against. The "passing grade" the manager must beat. For example, if a fixed deposit pays 2.5% and the fund returns 4.2%, the fund beat its benchmark by 1.7%. That extra return is called alpha. Consistent positive alpha proves the manager adds real value, not just luck.

What is an RSP?

RSP stands for Regular Savings Plan — an automatic monthly investment, like a standing order from your bank. Instead of trying to time the market, you invest RM200 every month no matter what. This builds wealth through ringgit cost averaging: you buy more units when prices are low and fewer when prices are high, reducing your average cost over time.

What is an e-Series Fund?

Public Mutual's e-series funds (prefixed with "Pe" — e.g., PeINCF, PeSukuk, PeIATGF) are their digital-first product range. They are designed for investors who prefer to start, manage, and top up investments online. The key advantage for Tan Aik Seng: lower minimum investment thresholds, making them ideal for starting with RM200/month. No branch visit required.
