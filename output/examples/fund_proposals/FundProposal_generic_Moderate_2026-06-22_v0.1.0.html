Before We Start — A Quick Foundation

You're new to unit trusts. Three short notes before we dive into the recommendations.

1. What is a Unit Trust?

A unit trust is a pool of money from many investors, professionally managed and invested across a basket of assets — stocks, bonds, cash — on your behalf. You own units; the fund manager does the work.

2. How Returns Work

Your investment grows in two ways: the price per unit (NAV) rises as the underlying assets appreciate, and some funds distribute income periodically. Reinvesting distributions compounds your growth.

3. Your Cooling-Off Right

As a first-time investor with Public Mutual, you have a 6 business day cooling-off period from the date of your first purchase. If you change your mind, you may redeem your units at the original NAV paid — no loss on the principal.

2. Distribution Policy

  • Reinvest distributionsreinvestAlways reinvest any income or dividends paid by your funds — compounding (earning returns on your returns) is the most powerful tool available over a 15-year horizon.
  • Avoid cash payoutsreinvestTaking distributions as cash feels like income but reduces the units you own, shrinking the base that compounds — keep every unit working for your retirement goal.
  • Growth phase priorityYou are in the accumulation phase of your investment journey, not the income phase — reinvesting maximises the probability of reaching your target by retirement.

3. Rebalancing Triggers

  • Annual reviewtime-basedCheck the portfolio's actual fund weightings once every 12 months and bring any drifted fund back to its target allocation to keep the risk level aligned with your Moderate profile.
  • Drift banddrift-basedIf any single fund's weighting moves more than 5% above or below its target — for example, PIEBF growing from 37.8% to above 42.8% — rebalance back to target regardless of the calendar.
  • Market shock triggerevent-basedAfter any major market event that moves global equities by more than 10% in a month, reassess all fund weightings — sharp rallies or crashes can quickly unbalance a portfolio.
  • Life milestone checkevent-basedAs retirement approaches within 3 years, gradually shift weighting toward the lower-risk money-market and gold funds to protect accumulated gains from a late-stage market fall.

4. Tactical Dip-Capture Playbook

  • Dip triggerthresholdIf PIEBF or PBF falls 10% or more from its recent high — the dip trigger (the price-drop threshold at which reserves get deployed) — switch a portion of PeCDF-A reserves into the falling fund to buy more units at a lower price.
  • Staged deploymentstagedDeploy reserve funds in two tranches — half at the 10% dip and the remaining half if the fund falls a further 5% — avoiding the risk of deploying all reserves in one move before the bottom is reached.
  • Reserve floorreserveAlways retain at least 5% of the total portfolio in PeCDF-A as a permanent liquidity buffer, even after dip deployments, so emergency funds remain accessible without forcing a sale of growth assets.
  • Normal volatilitythresholdDrops of less than 10% are normal price movement for balanced funds — do not deploy reserves or switch funds for routine fluctuations, as this disrupts the compounding process.
