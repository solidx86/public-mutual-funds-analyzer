Before We Start — A Quick Foundation

You're new to unit trusts. Three short notes before we dive into the recommendations.

1. What is a Unit Trust?

A unit trust is a pool of money from many investors, professionally managed and invested across a basket of assets — stocks, bonds, cash — on your behalf. You own units; the fund manager does the work.

2. How Returns Work

Your investment grows in two ways: the price per unit (NAV) rises as the underlying assets appreciate, and some funds distribute income periodically. Reinvesting distributions compounds your growth.

3. Your Cooling-Off Right

As a first-time investor with Public Mutual, you have a 6 business day cooling-off period from the date of your first purchase. If you change your mind, you may redeem your units at the original NAV paid — no loss on the principal.

2. Distribution Policy

  • Reinvest distributionsreinvestSet all fund distributions (income or dividends paid out by the fund) to auto-reinvest, so every payout buys more units and compounds your growth over the 15-year horizon.
  • Avoid early payoutsreinvestTaking cash distributions now reduces the number of units working for you — historically, compounding reinvested income accounts for a significant share of a balanced fund's total long-term return.
  • Review at age 60payoutSwitch to income-payout mode only as you approach retirement, when you need the portfolio to generate regular living expenses rather than continue growing.

3. Rebalancing Triggers

  • Annual reviewtime-basedCheck the portfolio's allocation weights every 12 months and top up or trim to restore the original split, even if markets have been quiet.
  • Drift banddrift-basedIf any single fund drifts more than 10% away from its target weight — for example, PIEBF rising from 37.8% to above 48% — rebalance back to target to prevent unintended concentration.
  • Market shock triggerevent-basedAfter any major market event causing a fund to drop more than 15% from recent highs, review whether to rebalance by moving reserves from PeCDF-A into the affected fund at lower prices.
  • Life-stage shiftevent-basedAt roughly the 10-year mark, begin gradually increasing the bond and money-market weighting to reduce volatility as your retirement date approaches.

4. Tactical Dip-Capture Playbook

  • Dip triggerthresholdWhen PIEBF or PBF falls 10% from its recent peak, deploy a portion of the PeCDF-A money-market reserve into the dipping fund — buying more units while prices are lower is the strategy, not a reason to exit.
  • Staged deploymentstagedSplit any reserve deployment across two or three tranches spaced 4–6 weeks apart rather than investing all at once — this avoids the risk of buying too early if the dip continues deeper.
  • Reserve floorreserveKeep at least 5% of the total portfolio in PeCDF-A at all times as a permanent liquidity cushion — never deploy the entire money-market balance, even in a deep correction.
  • Deep correctionthresholdA drop of 20% or more from a fund's all-time high (the highest price it has ever reached) is a strong signal to deploy a second, larger tranche — Moderate investors can tolerate this level of temporary loss in exchange for better long-term entry prices.
