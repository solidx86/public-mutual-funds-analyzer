Welcome — First Things First

This proposal is built for a first-time investor with a Moderate risk profile. Before the fund details, here is the foundation you need to read everything that follows.

What is a Unit Trust?

A unit trust is a pool of money from many investors, professionally managed and invested across a basket of assets — stocks, bonds, cash — on your behalf. You own units; the fund manager does the work of picking the underlying holdings.

How Does Your Investment Grow?

Your investment grows in two ways: the price per unit (NAV — the daily-quoted price of one unit of the fund) rises as the underlying assets appreciate, and some funds distribute income periodically (Public Enhanced Bond Fund paid 4.75 sen per unit in 2025). Reinvesting distributions compounds your growth tax-free in Malaysia.

Your Cooling-Off Right

As a first-time investor with Public Mutual, you have a 6 business day cooling-off period from the date of your first purchase. If you change your mind, you may redeem your units at the original NAV paid — you do not lose your principal during this window.

Realistic Expectations — What "Moderate" Means

Moderate means balanced growth with controlled risk. Your portfolio targets ~5% per year — meaningfully better than fixed deposits, with materially less volatility than a pure equity portfolio. Drawdowns of 5–10% during market stress are expected; drawdowns of 20%+ are not (those would suggest the portfolio is mis-calibrated to your profile). The investing horizon is 5+ years.

Why This Fund — And Why Alpha Is Not the Headline

This is a passive gold tracker, not an alpha play. The benchmark IS the gold spot price. Near-zero alpha means the fund is doing its job (efficient tracking). The slightly negative 3Y alpha (−0.04%) represents tracking cost, not manager failure. Including PeEMAS in your portfolio is a structural macro decision, not a manager-skill bet.
  • Inflation hedge. Gold historically appreciates when purchasing power erodes — a buffer if inflation reignites.
  • Decorrelation. Gold tends to rise when equities and bonds fall together — the 10% slice reduces the depth of any drawdown the equity portion of PESMACF and PEBF may experience.
  • MYR hedge. Held in USD-denominated ETFs (abrdn, GraniteShares, Goldman Sachs, iShares, VanEck) — partially offsets ringgit appreciation compressing your foreign-equity NAV.
  • Central-bank tailwind. Ongoing de-dollarisation by emerging-market central banks supports multi-year gold demand. The +34% trailing 1-year return reflects this structural force, not a speculative spike.

Macro Alignment

Middle East tensions, US Fed leadership transition (Powell → Warsh, May 2026), and ongoing China-US frictions are simultaneous risk drivers. Gold's role is to be the asset that does not correlate with the others — the part of the portfolio that holds steady or rises precisely when the equity and bond sleeves are stressed.

What to Watch

Risk Level 5 looks alarming on paper for a Moderate portfolio. The 10% sizing keeps the contribution to total-portfolio volatility modest, and the decorrelation benefit reduces overall drawdowns. Do not interpret the high RL as a recommendation against gold — interpret it as gold being volatile on its own, which is exactly why it has hedge value.

Investment Strategy & Tactical Playbook

Regular Savings Plan (RSP) — The Engine

Set up an automatic monthly investment (called a Regular Savings Plan, or RSP — like a standing order to your brokerage). Split it across the four funds in the same 35 / 35 / 10 / 20 ratio: 35% PESMACF, 35% PEBF, 10% PeEMAS, 20% PeCDF-A. RSP smooths your entry price (ringgit cost averaging — you buy more units when prices dip and fewer when prices are high), removes the need to time the market, and turns market volatility into your friend rather than your enemy.

Distribution Policy — Reinvest Everything

Reinvest all distributions across all four funds. Reason: distributions from Malaysian unit trusts to individual investors are tax-exempt; reinvesting compounds your alpha for free. PEBF distributed 4.75 sen per unit in 2025 — growing year-over-year; reinvestment lets that income compound rather than leak out of the portfolio.

Dip Capture Trigger — The Tactical Edge (Moderate Variant)

Trigger: If PESMACF or PEBF drops ≥5% from its all-time high (the highest NAV the fund has ever reached — check at publicmutual.com.my), execute the dip rule. Note: the threshold is lower than the 10% used for equity-heavy portfolios because Moderate funds drawdown less per cycle.

Action: Switch up to 50% of your PeCDF-A balance into the falling fund. Use the PMO PLUS app or contact your consultant.

Replenish: Direct the next 2–3 months of RSP entirely into PeCDF-A until the reserve is rebuilt to its target 20% allocation.

Why this works: It removes the emotional question of "is this the bottom?" The 5% threshold is the rule; the 50% deployment cap means you cannot go all-in on one dip and run out of ammunition for the next.

Rebalancing — Quarterly Cadence

Review the portfolio every 3 months. Rebalance back to target weights if any fund drifts more than 8 percentage points from its target (tighter than the 10pp threshold used for equity-heavy portfolios because Moderate has less natural drift). Major life events (job change, marriage, large windfall, planned major expense) trigger an unscheduled review.

6-Month Review Priority — Lifting Equity Exposure

If the client demonstrates comfort after living through one full market cycle, consider replacing PESMACF (RL 3, ~30% equity inside) with PIMXAF (Public Islamic Mixed Asset Fund — RL 4, AUM RM 1.3B, 3Y alpha +4.64%, 5Y +2.64%, ~65% equity inside). This shift would push the portfolio's look-through equity exposure from ~25% toward the upper bound (~35%) of the Moderate template, lift the target return delivery, and remain Shariah-aligned in spirit (PIMXAF is also Shariah-compliant). The trade-off is a higher Volatility Factor (from 3.8 to ~5.5 weighted) — appropriate once the client has built tolerance for normal market dips.

Cooling-Off Right — Your Safety Net

As a first-time individual investor with Public Mutual Berhad, you have 6 business days from the date of your initial investment to exercise the cooling-off right. If you change your mind within that window, your principal plus the sales charge are refunded. This applies to PESMACF, PEBF, PeEMAS, and PeCDF-A (confirmed in each fund's PHS).
