💰 Distribution Policy

Reinvest all distributions. Compounding is tax-exempt for Malaysian individuals — taking payouts is voluntarily forfeiting the most powerful force in long-term investing. PESMACF and PIATAF distributions reinvested into units amplify NAV growth compounding over a 5+ year horizon.

⚖️ Rebalancing Triggers
  • Time-based: Every 6 months — review allocation drift
  • Drift-based: Rebalance when any fund drifts >10% from target allocation
  • Event-based: Major life changes, BNM rate shift, market correction >20%
  • Next milestone: November 2026 — assess adding PBINFBF (top bond fund, CFS 45.7, 3Y alpha +2.69%) as a 5th fund once client is comfortable
🌱 New Investor Foundation
  • Cooling-off right: 6 business days from date of first investment — no penalty to exit within this window. Use this time to confirm your comfort with the portfolio before the first NAV movement.
  • What to expect in Year 1: Unit trust NAVs move daily. PESMACF will be stable (VF 3.5); PIATAF can move ±5–8% in a single week. Both are normal. The data in this proposal reflects performance over 3–10 year periods — short-term volatility is expected and is not a signal to exit.
  • Starting strategy: Make your initial investment at the target allocation, then set up the RSP immediately. The monthly RSP is more important than the lump-sum entry level — time in market beats timing the market over 5+ year horizons.
  • PIATAF risk reminder: A 10% correction in PIATAF is routine for an Asia tech fund. The dip capture rule is designed to make you buy more at that level — not to exit. Hold firm. The 3Y and 5Y alpha track record (+13.85% and +6.48%) demonstrates the manager recovers strongly.
  • 6-month review: After gaining comfort with the portfolio, discuss adding a dedicated bond fund (PBINFBF) or regional equity fund as portfolio slot #5 to normalise fixed income allocation and reduce PESMACF concentration.
