Before We Start — A Quick Foundation

You're new to unit trusts. Four short notes before we dive into the recommendations.

1. What is a Unit Trust?

A unit trust is a pool of money from many investors, professionally managed and invested across a basket of assets — stocks, bonds, cash — on your behalf. You own units; the fund manager does the work.

2. How Returns Work

Your investment grows in two ways: the price per unit (NAV) rises as the underlying assets appreciate, and some funds distribute income periodically. Reinvesting distributions compounds your growth.

3. Your Cooling-Off Right

As a first-time investor with Public Mutual, you have a 6 business day cooling-off period from the date of your first purchase. If you change your mind, you may redeem your units at the original NAV paid — no loss on the principal.

4. Why a Starter Portfolio

We're starting you with a focused 4-fund portfolio rather than a full 6-fund allocation. This keeps the moving parts low, and we can layer in additional funds at your next portfolio review once you're comfortable with the basics. Important: An Aggressive portfolio can drop 30–40% during market panics. This is expected and normal — the strategy is to hold through it, not to exit.

Investment Strategy

1. Regular Savings Plan (RSP)

Set up a standing monthly instruction in the same proportions as the portfolio allocation: 45% PIATAF / 25% PBGTHF / 18% PeEMAS / 12% PeCDF-A. Consistency matters more than timing — every month you buy some units higher and some lower (ringgit cost averaging), which works in your favour over time. Start with a comfortable monthly amount and scale up after the first 3-month review.

2. Distribution Policy

Default: reinvest all distributions. Compounding is tax-exempt for Malaysian individuals — reinvestment is more powerful than cash payouts during the growth phase. PeCDF-A pays monthly distributions; these can optionally be drawn as supplemental income later, but reinvest while building the portfolio. PIATAF and PBGTHF have low distribution frequency by design (growth-oriented).

3. Rebalancing Triggers

Quarterly review (every 3 months — Aggressive cadence). Drift rule: rebalance if any fund drifts >10% from its target allocation. Alpha alert: review immediately if PIATAF or PBGTHF 3Y alpha turns negative at a future MFR. Event-based: major life change, job change, or significant capital injection. 6-month mandatory review as a New Investor — confirm emotional comfort with the volatility you have actually experienced before expanding to additional funds.

4. Tactical Dip-Capture Playbook

When PIATAF or PBGTHF drops ≥10% from its most recent ATH, redeem up to 50% of PeCDF-A and switch into the dipping fund. After deployment, top up PeCDF-A over the next 2–3 months' RSP before the next trigger fires. Current status: PIATAF at ATH (no trigger); PBGTHF at −3.94% from ATH (163 days — approaching, not at, the 10% trigger). Do not exit during corrections — the strategy is designed for a 5+ year horizon and panic-selling forfeits the alpha thesis.
