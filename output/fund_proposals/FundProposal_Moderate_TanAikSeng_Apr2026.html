The Power of Starting at 28

RM1,000 invested per month at a modest 6% annualised return becomes approximately RM2.0 million by age 60. At 7% it becomes RM2.7 million. The mathematics of compounding reward time above all else. The best portfolio decision Tan will ever make is not which fund to choose — it is choosing to start today and staying the course for 30 years.

A drop of 20% in year 2 followed by a 25% recovery in year 3 is not a setback — it is a buying opportunity. Every bear market in the past 100 years has been followed by a new high. The only investors who lose permanently are those who panic-sell (sell out of fear during a downturn, locking in the loss) at the bottom and never return.

Suggested RSP (Regular Savings Plan) Allocation

An RSP is a standing monthly investment instruction — the same amount moves automatically from your bank account into your chosen funds each month, regardless of market conditions. This removes the psychological burden of timing the market and builds wealth systematically.

Guidance for a New Investor

Check your portfolio once a month — not daily. Daily price checking creates anxiety without adding information. Fund prices fluctuate every day; the signal that matters is the 3-month and 12-month trend.

Do not redeem when the market falls. The urge to "stop the bleeding" by selling during a downturn is the most expensive mistake a new investor can make. The funds selected here have recovered from every previous downturn. The question is not whether they will recover — it is whether you will still be holding when they do.

Ignore noise. Financial news is designed to generate emotion, not to help you invest. If a headline says "Market Crashes 8%" — check that your RSP is still running and do nothing else. If your money market reserve is above 10% of portfolio, consider deploying the dip protocol.
