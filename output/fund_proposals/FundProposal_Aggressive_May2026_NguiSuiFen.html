Investment Strategy

1. Regular Savings Plan (RSP)

Contribute monthly in proportion to target weights — PIATAF (45%) as the primary growth engine, PITSEQ (20%) as the Malaysia anchor, PHGEF (13%) accumulating through the current healthcare dip, PeEMAS (10%) maintaining the structural hedge, and PeCDF-A (12%) rebuilding after each tactical deployment. RSP smooths entry timing and is the default execution mode for the 5+ year Aggressive horizon.

2. Distribution Policy

Reinvest all distributions (default for Aggressive). Maximum compounding over the long horizon — Malaysia unit-trust income distributions are tax-exempt at the unitholder level, so reinvestment amplifies the compounding effect rather than triggering tax leakage.

3. Rebalancing Triggers

Review quarterly (Aggressive cadence). Drift trigger: any fund drifting >10% from target allocation prompts a switch from the over-weight to the under-weight fund. Event triggers: broad-market correction >15% activates dip capture; new MFR data prompts a re-screening pass; any life event (job change, major income shift, property purchase) prompts a profile re-assessment.

4. Tactical Dip-Capture Playbook

When PIATAF, PITSEQ, or PHGEF drops ≥10% from its most recent ATH, deploy up to 50% of the PeCDF-A reserve into the dipping fund — leaving reserve for a deeper second leg. PHGEF currently at −10.32% (170 days from ATH) is already at the trigger boundary; flag for systematic accumulation. Replenish the PeCDF-A reserve by increasing its RSP contribution for 2–3 months after each deployment.
