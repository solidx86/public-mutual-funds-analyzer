Welcome — First Things First

This proposal is built for a first-time investor. Before the fund details, here is the foundation you need to read everything that follows.

What is a Unit Trust?

A unit trust is a pool of money from many investors, professionally managed and invested across a basket of assets — stocks, bonds, cash — on your behalf. You own units; the fund manager does the work of picking and timing the underlying holdings.

How Does Your Investment Grow?

Your investment grows in two ways: the price per unit (NAV — the daily-quoted price of one unit of the fund) rises as the underlying assets appreciate, and some funds distribute income periodically. Reinvesting distributions compounds your growth tax-free in Malaysia.

Your Cooling-Off Right

As a first-time investor with Public Mutual, you have a 6 business day cooling-off period from the date of your first purchase. If you change your mind, you may redeem your units at the original NAV paid — you do not lose your principal during this window.

Realistic Expectations

Unit trusts are medium-to-long term investments (5+ years). Short-term price fluctuations are normal — the data in this proposal reflects historical performance over full market cycles, which is the timeframe that actually matters. A drawdown (a fall from a recent high) of 10–20% during the holding period is expected, not a sign that something has gone wrong.

Why This Fund — And Why Alpha Is Not the Headline

This is a passive gold tracker, not an alpha play. The benchmark IS the gold spot price. Near-zero alpha means the fund is doing its job (efficient tracking). The slightly negative 3Y alpha (−0.04%) represents tracking cost, not manager failure. Including PeEMAS in your portfolio is a structural macro decision, not a manager-skill bet.
  • Inflation hedge. Gold historically appreciates when purchasing power erodes — a buffer against unexpected inflation reigniting.
  • Decorrelation. Gold tends to rise when equities fall — the 10% slice reduces the depth of any drawdown the equity portion may experience.
  • MYR hedge. Held in USD-denominated ETFs (abrdn, GraniteShares, Goldman Sachs, iShares, VanEck) — partially offsets ringgit appreciation compressing your foreign-equity NAV.

Investment Strategy & Tactical Playbook

Regular Savings Plan (RSP) — The Engine

Set up an automatic monthly investment (called a Regular Savings Plan, or RSP — like a standing order to your brokerage). Split it across the four funds in the same 40 / 35 / 10 / 15 ratio: 40% PITSEQ, 35% PSSF, 10% PeEMAS, 15% PeCDF-A. RSP smooths your entry price (ringgit cost averaging — you buy more units when prices dip and fewer when prices are high), removes the need to time the market, and turns market volatility into your friend rather than your enemy.

Distribution Policy — Reinvest Everything

Reinvest all distributions across all four funds. Reason: distributions from Malaysian unit trusts to individual investors are tax-exempt; reinvesting compounds your alpha for free. You can switch to "payout" later if income becomes a goal — but at the wealth-accumulation stage, compound everything.

Dip Capture Trigger — The Tactical Edge

Trigger: If PITSEQ or PSSF drops ≥10% from its all-time high (the highest NAV the fund has ever reached — check at publicmutual.com.my), execute the dip rule.

Action: Switch up to 50% of your PeCDF-A balance into the falling fund. Use the PMO PLUS app or contact your consultant.

Replenish: Direct the next 2–3 months of RSP entirely into PeCDF-A until the reserve is rebuilt to its target 15% allocation.

Why this works: It removes the emotional question of "is this the bottom?" The 10% threshold is the rule; the 50% deployment cap means you cannot go all-in on one dip and run out of ammunition for the next.

Rebalancing — Quarterly Cadence

Review the portfolio every 3 months. Rebalance back to target weights if any fund drifts more than 10 percentage points from its target (e.g., PITSEQ at 50%+ from a 40% target, or PeCDF-A below 5% from a 15% target). Major life events (job change, marriage, large windfall, planned major expense) trigger an unscheduled review.

6-Month Review Priority — Adding the Alpha Outlier

At the 6-month review, evaluate adding PIATAF (Public Islamic Asia Tactical Allocation Fund) as an Alpha Outlier satellite at 10–15% allocation, carved from PSSF or PITSEQ. PIATAF's 3Y alpha of +13.85% per year is the highest in the entire qualified universe, and its top holdings (Samsung, TSMC, SK Hynix) provide direct Asia tech exposure. It is intentionally not in the starter portfolio because RL 5 / VF 22.4 means 30%+ drawdowns are normal — the wrong first experience for a new investor. After the client has lived through one mid-cycle dip and stayed invested, PIATAF becomes appropriate.

Cooling-Off Right — Your Safety Net

As a first-time individual investor with Public Mutual Berhad, you have 6 business days from the date of your initial investment to exercise the cooling-off right. If you change your mind within that window, your principal plus the sales charge are refunded. This applies to PITSEQ, PSSF, PeEMAS, and PeCDF-A (confirmed in each fund's PHS).
