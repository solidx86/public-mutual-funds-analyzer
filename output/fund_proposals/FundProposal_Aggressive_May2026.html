Why Gold — The "So What"
Gold is not selected for manager skill — it is selected for what it does to the portfolio's overall risk profile. Gold has near-zero (and sometimes negative) correlation to Asian equity. When PIATAF and PBGTHF fall 30% during a market panic, gold typically rises or stays flat. This reduces the depth of your worst-case loss even in a portfolio with no Fixed Income (FI/Sukuk) buffer.

The 3Y alpha of −0.04% is perfectly acceptable — it means the fund tracks the gold spot price almost exactly. That is exactly what a gold fund should do. The Benchmark IS the gold price; near-zero alpha = efficient tracking, not underperformance.

Three structural roles:
(1) Inflation hedge: Gold historically appreciates when purchasing power is eroded — particularly relevant with tariff-driven goods inflation currently in play.
(2) MYR hedge: Gold is priced in USD. As MYR strengthens (currently near 8-year highs), this partially offsets the foreign equity NAV compression on PIATAF and PBGTHF.
(3) Decorrelation: Reduces the portfolio's maximum drawdown depth during equity crashes.
Why 18% — Higher Than Typical Template

The standard Aggressive template allocates 8–12% to gold. This portfolio allocates 18% because there is zero Fixed Income / Sukuk in the portfolio — by design for an Aggressive profile. Gold steps in as the sole non-equity hedge. A higher gold weight compensates for the absent FI buffer without sacrificing the aggressive growth thesis. Top holdings: abrdn Physical Gold Shares ETF, GraniteShares Gold Trust, Goldman Sachs Physical Gold ETF, iShares Gold Trust, VanEck Merk Gold ETF — five physically-backed gold ETFs for pure gold exposure.

What to Watch
⚠ PeEMAS is currently −8.34% from its January 29, 2026 ATH (76 days ago). This is normal retracement after a strong run. Gold VF of 13.0 is lower than both PIATAF (22.4) and PBGTHF (15.5) — at 18% weight, gold actively moderates the portfolio's overall volatility.
