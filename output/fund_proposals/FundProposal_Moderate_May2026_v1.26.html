Before We Start — A Quick Foundation

You're new to unit trusts. Four short notes before we dive into the recommendations.

1. What is a Unit Trust?

A unit trust is a pool of money from many investors, professionally managed and invested across a basket of assets — stocks, bonds, cash — on your behalf. You own units; the fund manager does the work.

2. How Returns Work

Your investment grows in two ways: the price per unit (NAV) rises as the underlying assets appreciate, and some funds distribute income periodically. Reinvesting distributions compounds your growth.

3. Your Cooling-Off Right

As a first-time investor with Public Mutual, you have a 6 business day cooling-off period from the date of your first purchase. If you change your mind, you may redeem your units at the original NAV paid — no loss on the principal.

4. Why a Starter Portfolio

We're starting you with a focused 4-fund portfolio rather than a full 6-fund allocation. This keeps the moving parts low, and we can layer in additional funds at your next portfolio review once you're comfortable with the basics.

Investment Strategy

1. Regular Savings Plan (RSP)

Set up an automatic monthly investment (an RSP — like a standing order to your brokerage). Split it across the four funds in the same 35 / 35 / 10 / 20 ratio: 35% PESMACF, 35% PEBF, 10% PeEMAS, 20% PeCDF-A. RSP smooths your entry price (ringgit cost averaging), removes the need to time the market, and turns market volatility into your friend rather than your enemy.

2. Distribution Policy

Reinvest all distributions across all four funds. Distributions from Malaysian unit trusts to individual investors are tax-exempt; reinvesting compounds your alpha for free. PEBF distributed 4.75 sen per unit in 2025 — growing year-over-year — and reinvestment lets that income compound rather than leak out of the portfolio.

3. Rebalancing Triggers

Review the portfolio every 3 months. Rebalance back to target weights if any fund drifts more than 8 percentage points from its target (tighter than the 10pp threshold used for equity-heavy portfolios because Moderate has less natural drift). Major life events (job change, marriage, large windfall, planned major expense) trigger an unscheduled review. At the 6-month review, consider replacing PESMACF with PIMXAF (RL 4, ~65% equity inside) to lift look-through equity toward the upper bound of the Moderate template once the client has built tolerance.

4. Tactical Dip-Capture Playbook

Trigger: if PESMACF or PEBF drops ≥5% from its all-time high (check NAV at publicmutual.com.my), switch up to 50% of your PeCDF-A balance into the falling fund. The 5% threshold is lower than the 10% used for equity-heavy portfolios because Moderate funds drawdown less per cycle. Replenish: direct the next 2–3 months of RSP entirely into PeCDF-A until the reserve is rebuilt to its target 20% allocation. Why this works: it removes the emotional question of "is this the bottom?" — the 5% threshold is the rule, and the 50% deployment cap means you cannot go all-in on one dip and run out of ammunition for the next.
